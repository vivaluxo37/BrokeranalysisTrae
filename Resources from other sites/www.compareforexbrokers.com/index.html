Why traders should compare Forex brokers?

Choosing the right forex broker is the second most important factor to successful trading; only your skills as a trader are more important. Good brokers provide the right infrastructure for you to execute your trading plan and give you the best chance of succeeding. Trading conditions provided by the broker to look for include low costs and fees, fast execution, a wide range of instruments, top trading platforms along with other features like automation, copy trading and social trading.

Broker Fees

The main fee a broker charges is the spread which is the difference between the ask and sell price for any currency pair. Our research found that spreads can be up to 300% higher between the most competitive brokers and expensive ones.

The second fee is the commission which applies to RAW/ECN accounts. In this case, the broker with the lowest fee had half the commission rate of the most expensive provider. The commission rate varies also by the base currency a trader selects when opening an account.

Trading Platforms

Most brokers offer a number of mainstream forex platforms such as MetaTrader 4, MetaTrader 5, cTrader and TradingView. If you’re used to one of these platforms, then you should visit our broker-by-platform pages. Other options include proprietary platforms which often have features not found in mainstream platforms such as a guaranteed stop loss, extra pending order types and charts.

Broker Performance

The technology stack of each broker can impact factors such as slippage. Based on this, we tested each broker’s execution speed over two 24-hour periods so traders can understand the performance of each provider’s platform, liquidity and servers.

Trading Conditions

Brokers and often their regulators determine what can be traded and under what conditions. This includes leverage for retail traders, which can range from a maximum of 30:1 to 500:1. Regulation rules can also determine what type of trading is available, for example, in the UK, only some brokers offer financial spreads betting accounts. These trading conditions can often be tested on a demo account first before trading with real money. There is also a large variation in regard to the minimum deposit requirements to start trading on a live account.

Range Of Markets

While all providers offer currency trading on major currency pairs including EUR/USD, AUD/USD, EUR/USD and USD/JPY they will vary when it comes to minor and exotic pairs. There is also variation when it comes to Share CFDs, commodities, indices, EFTs and crypto CFDs.

How do you get started trading forex in Australia?

To get started trading forex in Australia, we recommend a measured approach based on research and extensive testing.

Choose a
forex broker

Take into account your experience level, budget and overall goals for forex trading. Ensure that the broker offers access to currency pairs you want to trade and provides the tools and platforms you need for technical analysis.

Refine your
trading strategy

If your preferred broker offers a demo account, take this opportunity to test out different platforms to ensure that you’re comfortable with the interface and trading tools available

We also suggest taking it slow. Backtest your strategies extensively and open small positions while you finetune your ideas. Emotional overtrading will cost you more money than poor market analysis.

management tools

Once you understand your entry and exit points, take measures to limit your exposure and control your risk. Popular methods include hedging, stop loss and limit orders and, where available, guaranteed stop loss orders.

FAQs

What is ASIC in Forex?

The Australia Securities and Investment Commission (ASIC) regulates Australia’s financial markets, securities and investment industry to ensure fairness and transparency.

Is Forex trading legal in Australia?

Yes, Forex trading is legal for Australian citizens and residents. If you use a broker regulated by the Australian Securities and Investments Commission (ASIC), you can be confident the broker complies with Corporations Act of 2001.

Do you pay taxes when forex trading in Australia?

Yes, you pay taxes when forex trading in Australia. As a trader, you will be liable to Capital gains of 50% of your marginal tax rate. If you trade Forex as a business owner, profits may be considered business income rather than capital. In this case, all profits are taxed at your current rate.

What Is Forex Trading?

Forex trading involves buying one currency with another and profiting from the relative price difference. For example, if you think the US dollar will increase in value against the euro, you might buy dollars with euros. If the dollar appreciates on a relative basis, you can sell your dollars back for more than you spent to buy them.

Understanding The Risks Of Forex Trading

Currency trading is a leveraged product, which means that an FX broker can amplify your deposit by up to 500 (500:1) times in some regions. This leverage means there is a high risk of losing your money when financial markets become volatile. It is important to understand the risks and disclaimers involved and consider options such as demo accounts prior to trading real money.
